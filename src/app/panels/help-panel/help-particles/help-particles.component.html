Fermions

Fermions are fundamental particles. They are not made up from anything else. However they can transform into smaller fermions via various decay processes.

There are several classes of Fermion. Those you have discovered so far are shown below:

Quarks

u, u̅ Up quark and up antiquark, the smallest of the quarks
d, d̅ Down quark and down antiquark, another small quark
s, s̅ Strange quark and antiquark
c, c̅ Charm quark and antiquark
b, b̅ Bottom quark and antiquark
t, t̅ Top quark and antiquark - by far the largest quark, also the least stable

Leptons

e⁻, e⁺ Electrons and their positively charged antiparticles Positrons
μ⁻, μ⁺ Muons and antimuons, slightly larger than electrons
τ⁻, τ⁺ Tau leptons, or tauons, and antitau leptons, slightly larger than muons
νe, ν̅e Electron neutrinos and antineutrinos
νμ, ν̅μ Muon neutrinos and antineutrinos
ντ, ν̅τ Tau neutrinos and antineutrinos

Gauge Bosons

γ Photons, the vector of electromagnetic energy
g Gluons, the vector of the Strong Interaction force
W⁺, W⁻ W bosons, a vector of the Weak Interaction force
Z The Z boson, a vector of the Weak Interaction force
